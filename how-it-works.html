FAQ
Common questions
Everything shippers ask before their first booking.
Do I need a KVK or VAT registration to book?
Yes. CorridorBook is a B2B freight platform. You need a valid business registration (KVK in NL, KBO in BE, HRB in DE or equivalent) and a VAT number to complete a booking. Individual shippers without registration should contact their freight forwarder.
How long does the slot hold last?
Once you click "Select & Book", a 20-minute hold is placed on the slot. This gives you time to review the booking details and confirm payment. If the timer expires, the slot is released back to the market and you return to search results.
Can I book multiple containers or mixed loads?
Yes. Use the quantity field on the search form to book multiple containers of the same type. For mixed loads (e.g. 2 × 40HC + 1 × 20GP), create separate bookings per container type. Bulk booking for 10+ containers is available on request via the operators panel.
What container types are supported?
CorridorBook currently supports: 20ft General Purpose (20GP), 40ft General Purpose (40GP), 40ft High Cube (40HC), 45ft High Cube (45HC), 20ft Reefer (20RF) and 40ft Reefer (40RF). Dangerous goods (ADN/ADR certified), open-top and flat-rack are available via selected operators — contact them directly via the "Operator info" button on the results screen.
How are CO₂ savings calculated?
We use the GLEC Framework (Global Logistics Emissions Council) emission factors: road HGV ~120 g CO₂/tonne·km, inland waterway ~38 g CO₂/tonne·km, rail ~26 g CO₂/tonne·km. Savings are calculated against the road baseline for your specific cargo weight and distance. The figures shown are indicative — actual emissions depend on vessel load factor, fuel type and route.
What if I need to cancel a booking?
You can cancel up to 24 hours before the gate-open time. Cancellation fees apply: 8% for road, 12% for rail, 15% for barge. The booking reference page shows your exact cancellation deadline and fee. After the deadline, the full freight charge applies.
How does live tracking work?
After booking, your shipment appears in My Shipments with a live milestone tracker. Barge positions are updated via AIS (Automatic Identification System). Rail events come from the operator's TMS feed. Road shipments are tracked via driver app GPS. Update frequency: every 5–15 minutes depending on mode and operator.
